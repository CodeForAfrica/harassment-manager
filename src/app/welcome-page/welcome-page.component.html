FAQs

Why do I have to register to use FeedShield?

By registering, you’ll help us understand who is using the tool, so we can constantly improve FeedShield’s capabilities to suit your needs.

You can check out our privacy policy to learn more about which personal information we collect and how it's protected.

How does FeedShield work?

FeedShield syncs with your Twitter account and uses machine learning technology to identify abusive comments. The tool rates each comment with a toxicity score and lets you take action - by muting or blocking accounts in bulk - without having to read the abusive messages. While all comments are automatically hidden, you can unhide them if you want to.

You can also collate harmful comments into a single report. This report can be downloaded and shared with Twitter and other trusted third parties as needed.

Abusive accounts will not know that they have been featured in a report. No third party - including Code for Africa and Twitter - will be able to access any of the reports you create, unless shared directly with them. Draft reports are temporarily stored in the tool to enable you to complete them. Once downloaded or abandoned, the report will be deleted from FeedShield.

How is my data protected

Yes, your data is kept private when you use FeedShield. We only collect essential information such as your name and email address, and we will never share your personal data with third parties without your consent. We only use your personal data for the purpose for which it was collected.

What languages does the tool work with?

The FeedShield Tool is currently available in English. We are working on French and Arabic languages, so please check back soon for updates.

Can FeedShield identify harmful images and videos?

FeedShield is not designed to identify harmful images and videos. However, the tool does allow users to report any inappropriate or offensive content.

Are Direct Messages (DMs) analysed by the tool?

No, the tool only analyses public tweets.

Is FeedShield free to use?

Yes, Feedshield is absolutely free to use.

What are the future development plans for FeedShield?

FeedShield will initially tackle trolling on Twitter, with other social media platforms added as access to the relevant data becomes available. CfA’s initial launch will focus on Kenya, and will expand to offer support to users elsewhere in Africa from 2023. Arabic and Francophone versions of the tool will launch in 2023.

Is FeedShield available on my mobile device?

FeedShield is accessible on your smartphone, but is optimised for desktop use.

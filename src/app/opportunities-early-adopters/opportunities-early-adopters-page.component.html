Call for Applications for Early Adopters to Combat Online Gender-Based Harassment

Oct 26, 2022 | Fellows , Africa

Do trolls invade your timeline all too often? Want to filter them out before you read them?

FeedShield, a tool developed by Jigsaw and implemented by Code for Africa (CfA), allows you to do just that. With FieldShield, you can sort your mentions based on how likely they are to be toxic or harmful. You can then package together harmful posts to send to social media support teams so they can take action against toxic accounts and trolls.

As part of our launch, we are offering fellowships to women journalists and fact-checkers across a number of African countries who want to be some of the first users to roll out this new tool.

We’re looking for early adopters in one of these countries, who will be part of the first roll out: Burkina Faso, Burundi, Cameroon, the Central African Republic (CAR), Chad, Côte d’Ivoire, the Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC), Ethiopia, Ghana, Kenya, Mali, Niger, Nigeria, Senegal, Sierra Leone, Somalia, South Africa, South Sudan, Sudan, Tanzania, and Zimbabwe.

Along with access to the tool, the chosen fellows will receive a financial stipend and access to CfA’s investigative team who, if the fellow wants, can investigate if the harassment they experience is coordinated.

In return, fellows will need to document their journey as FeedShield’s'early adopters’, demonstrating the tool’s effectiveness to their followers and networks.

Who can apply?

Women journalists and human rights activists that work in African countries, or that are affiliated with African-based partners can apply.

All applications will be evaluated by an internal review committee.

Details about the fellowship include:

  • Duration: 2 months
  • Stipend: Fellows will receive a substantive monthly stipend
  • Support
    • Fellows will have access to a crisis helpline for emergency digital counselling.
    • Fellows can request an investigation on online harassment from CfA’s investigative team.
    • Fellows will be invited to trainings on online digital security principles and other related workshops.

Are You Eligible?

The Early Adopters Fellowships are open to all women journalists and fact-checkers in the 21 countries listed above.

Therefore applicants must meet the following criteria:

  • You are a journalist or fact-checker working on the continent.
  • You must be non-partisan, without any link to political parties or government agencies or paramilitary organisations or state-affiliated media or other bodies that might create the perception of partisanship in the elections..
  • You must be able to commit to being an ambassador for the tool, showcasing its usefulness and effectiveness to your networks and followers.
  • You must be fluent in English and, preferably, any local languages spoken in the 21 target countries.
  • You must have access to a reliable home laptop/computer with stable internet connectivity, to enable you to meet programme metrics.
  • You must commit to using your social media channels to amplify the activities and work produced during the fellowship.

It will be advantageous if you have:

  • A substantive following/audience on social media.
  • A compelling portfolio for viral visual content creation that has built meaningful audience engagement.

The deadline for applications is 01 January 2023

Who manages and funds FeedShield?

Code for Africa is the continent’s largest network of civic technology and data journalism labs, with teams in 21 countries. CfA builds digital democracy solutions that give citizens unfettered access to actionable information that empowers them to make informed decisions, and that strengthens civic engagement for improved public governance and accountability. This includes building and managing investigative and analytical infrastructure, like the PesaCheck fact-checking initiative, the African Network of Centers for Investigative Reporting (ANCIR), the frican Fact Checking AllianceA (AFCA), nd the AfricanDRONE network of drone journalists, the sensor.AFRICA network of citizen scientists, and the WanaData network of women data scientists/storytellers.

CfA is funded by a variety of organisations including, amongst others, the Deutsche Welle Akademie (DWA) and the International Center for Journalism (ICFJ), the UNDP and UNESCO, the Pulitzer Center on Crisis Reporting, Jigsaw, the International Fact-Checking Network (IFCN), Meta’s Journalism Programme, the Agence Française de Développement (AFD), the World Bank, the US State Department, the Deutsche Gesellschaft für Internationale Zusammenarbeit (GIZ) and the BMZ (German Federal Ministry for Economic Cooperation and Development).

This specific fellowship programme is offered by CfA in partnership with Jigsaw:

Jigsaw is a unit within Google that explores threats to open societies, and builds technology that inspires scalable solutions. Based in New York City, Jigsaw is dedicated to understanding global challenges and applying technological solutions, from "countering extremism", online censorship and cyber-attacks, to protecting access to information.
